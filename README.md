# 🛒 AtliQ Mart — Promotional Campaign Analysis

## 📌 Problem Statement

AtliQ Mart, a retail supermarket chain, conducted promotional campaigns during the Diwali 2024 and Sankranti 2024 festive seasons.  
The business wants to understand which promotions were effective in increasing sales and how future campaigns can be optimized.

This project analyzes campaign performance across promotions, products, categories, and cities to support data-driven decision-making.

---

## 📊 Dataset Overview

The dataset contains promotional sales events across multiple stores and cities, including:

- 1,500 sales events  
- 2 campaigns (Diwali & Sankranti)  
- 5 promotion types  
- 50 stores across 10 cities  
- 15 products across multiple categories  

Key fields include:

- Quantity sold before promotion  
- Quantity sold after promotion  
- Promotion type  
- Campaign details  
- Product category  
- Store location  

---

## 🎯 Business Questions

This analysis aims to answer:

- Which promotion types drove the highest sales growth?
- Which campaign performed best?
- Which products and categories contributed most?
- Which cities responded strongest to promotions?
- Which promotions should be continued or discontinued?

---

## 🔍 Key Insights

- 🏆 **BOGOF delivered the highest growth (+270%)**, adding ~157K incremental units  
- 💰 **₹500 Cashback performed strongly (+183%)**, especially for premium products  
- ❌ **25% OFF resulted in negative growth (−13%)** — ineffective promotion  
- 🎉 **Sankranti campaign outperformed Diwali** significantly (155% vs 66%)  
- 🏙️ **Tier-1 cities (Bengaluru, Chennai, Hyderabad)** drove the majority of sales  
- 🛒 Grocery & Staples category dominated promotional response  

---

## 💡 Business Recommendations

- Use **BOGOF for high-volume essential products**
- Apply **cashback offers for premium or bundled items**
- Avoid shallow discounts like 25% OFF
- Allocate more marketing budget to Tier-1 cities
- Replicate Sankranti campaign strategy in future promotions
